## Why this matters

Most people open Claude, type a message, close the tab. That's 1% of what it's good for. Before we get into the fun stuff — projects, Cowork, agents, the AI OS — you need the fundamentals nailed down. Models, plans, desktop app, memory, artifacts. Get these right on Day 2 and everything from here on gets faster.

## Step-by-step

1. **Learn the three models.** Drop the model selector in the bottom right of the text box and you'll see three brains built for different jobs:
   - **Opus** — the specialist. Deep reasoning, coding, hard problems. Slower, more expensive.
   - **Sonnet** — your daily driver. 90% of what you'll do runs here. Best speed-versus-intelligence balance.
   - **Haiku** — quick, cheap, bulk. Snappy judgments, not a heavy thinker.
   - Default rule: **use Sonnet. Escalate to Opus when answers feel shallow. Drop to Haiku for quick or bulk stuff.**
2. **Know when to flip on Extended Thinking.** Turn this on when you want Claude to pause and reason step by step through a decision or a build. It's slower and more expensive, so don't use it for everyday stuff — but for big thinking tasks, it's worth it.
3. **Pick a plan.** Go to `claude.ai/upgrade`. Two options:
   - **Pro** (~$20/month) — start here. Covers 99% of what you need early on.
   - **Max** ($100 or $200 tier) — Samin runs the $200 tier and pulls in the API equivalent of $3k–$5k/month of usage. Worth it if you're pacing your usage. Don't cram.
   - Don't use the pay-as-you-go API. It gets expensive super fast. That's for developers wiring Claude into software, not for everyday use.
4. **Install the Claude Desktop app.** In the bottom left of Claude.ai, there's a small download icon. Hit it, download for your OS, install, open, log in with the same account. 80% of this course runs through the desktop app — that's where Cowork, Claude Code, and all the power tools live.
   - Make sure your Mac/Windows is on a recent OS version. The desktop app moves fast and the new features only show up if your machine is current.
5. **Turn on memory.** Once in the desktop app: bottom left → settings → Capabilities → flip on both memory toggles. This lets Claude remember your preferences, ongoing projects, and how you like things done across every conversation. Stops you from repeating yourself.
6. **Turn on artifacts.** Same Capabilities screen, scroll down, check everything under artifacts. Artifacts are how Claude builds documents, diagrams, and interactive tools on a side panel instead of dumping a wall of text at you. We'll lean on these hard starting Day 3.
7. **Set up voice (optional but recommended).** The desktop app has a voice button in the prompt bar. Samin uses **Whisperflow** system-wide — hold a key, talk, it drops what you said into whatever app you're in. Typing is fine. Voice is faster and you think clearer on paper.

## Try it yourself

Open settings right now. Flip on memory and artifacts. Hit new chat. Say "remember that I prefer bullet points over long paragraphs." Close the chat. Open a new one. Ask anything. See if it remembers. Screenshot that and drop it in `artifact/`.

## Common stuck points

- **Defaulting to Opus for everything.** Opus is slow and burns tokens. You'll hit your rate limits fast. Sonnet handles 90% of tasks.
- **Using the browser instead of the desktop app.** The browser is fine to start, but Cowork, Claude Code, and most of the power features only show up on desktop. If you stay on the browser, you'll keep asking "why can't I find this button?"
- **Paying for Max too early.** Start on Pro. Only upgrade when you're actually pacing against the ceiling. Don't pay for a tier you won't use yet.
- **Skipping the OS update.** The desktop app moves faster than most software. If your machine is from five years ago and hasn't been updated, the newest features won't show up. Update first.

## Key takeaways

- Three models, one rule: Sonnet by default, Opus when you need deeper thinking, Haiku for bulk or speed.
- 80% of this course runs on the Claude Desktop app. Download it today.
- Memory + artifacts must be on. That's non-negotiable for the rest of the course.
- Pro plan is fine to start. Don't jump to Max until you're hitting limits.
- Voice input (Whisperflow or the built-in voice button) is way faster than typing — and you'll think clearer out loud.
