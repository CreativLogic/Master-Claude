## Why this matters

Yesterday we started the Scan step by hand. Today we finish all five letters of SHIPS and actually turn one of your top priorities into a skill that runs on a schedule. This is the single most valuable output of the course — if you do nothing else, do this. Samin's words: "if you just get this report, then you know what to automate, or even if you don't want to automate it, you know who to hire to take it over." That's how you buy back your time.

## Step-by-step

1. **Finish the Scan step with software, not a spreadsheet.** Two options. **ScreenPipe** records your whole screen and audio locally — tell Claude "hey, set this up for me" and it installs. **RescueTime** is easier if you're not super technical — go for the $12 bundle with time sheets. Dump the time sheets into Claude with the priority matrix prompt and you get a real report back.
2. **H — Hook up everything.** Inside Claude go to Customize → Skills and Connectors. Every tool you use, every person in your life, every service — make sure Claude has the context. You've been doing this the whole course.
3. **I — Identify just three.** Take your priority matrix, pick the top three, forget everything else. Don't try to automate 20 things. Three. That's the entire Identify step.
4. **P — Package each one into a skill on a schedule.** Remember the Instagram carousel you built earlier in the course? Same move. Turn the task into a skill, set a schedule, let Claude do it on its own. Schedule skills like Samin's "evolving brain sync" are the pattern.
5. **S — Scale by having Claude suggest more.** Set up a cron job: "Every day (or every week), look at what I've been doing, and give me a report on the things I can automate that would be highest ROI." Samin's research scheduler does this — goes to Twitter, pulls Karpathy's newest posts, finds SEO agents, and suggests the next thing to automate. Automation suggesting more automation.
6. **Do a real example — Alex's ad creation.** Samin grabs the "ecom ads" skill from his community's Claude Skills Vault, drops it into Claude Code, pastes a product photo (Alex's pink bottle), and says: "Hey, can you use the ecom ad skill I just put in here. For this product create some image ads for me, please. Make a new folder, Alex Pink Bottle." Hit go.
7. **Put that skill on a schedule and connect it to Meta Ads.** Four ads come back — bottle in product shots, copy written, assets pulled from the internet, Nano Banana Pro used for the image composites. Now say "put this on a schedule and make ads every hour." Then open Composio, connect your Meta Ads account, and Claude can see which ads perform, make new ones, and keep iterating on autopilot. Static ad creation — gone. That's a real priority task deleted.

## Try it yourself

Install RescueTime (or ScreenPipe if you're technical), let it record for a few hours, then dump the time sheet into Claude with the priority matrix prompt. Pick the top result. Turn it into a skill before you close the laptop.

## Common stuck points

- Trying to automate 10 things at once. Pick three. That's the Identify step, that's the whole rule.
- Skipping the Hook up step. If Claude doesn't have context — skills, connectors, who your people are — your skills will do generic work.
- Building a skill but forgetting the schedule. A skill that runs once is a script. A skill on a cron is an agent.
- Thinking Scan is a one-time thing. Set up the Scale cron so Claude keeps suggesting new automations from your actual screen time.

## Key takeaways

- SHIPS: **S**can your workflow, **H**ook up your tools, **I**dentify top three, **P**ackage into skills on schedules, **S**cale by having Claude suggest more.
- The priority matrix report is the single most valuable artifact of this whole course. Even if you don't automate, you'll know who to hire.
- Every automated task = a skill + a schedule. That's the pattern. Repeat it for everything.
- Skills from your community (or online) are the heavy lifting. Download one, point it at your product, get results. You don't build from scratch.
- Connect Composio at the end so Claude reads performance data and iterates ads (or emails, or posts) on autopilot.
